When the website Healthcare.gov launched a few years ago, there were a variety of reasons why people had a difficult time signing up for health care via the site. One of the most common complaints people had with the on-boarding process was the overall user experience. For example, the largest calls to action on the original site consisted of two buttons that said 'Apply Online' and 'Apply By Phone'. Neither option would've been helpful to the person who was unfamiliar with the registration process and rollout of the site.

As a side project I created wireframes of how Healthcare.gov could have improved its on-boarding process. The wireframes walk through a mobile version of the site, as I designed the user flow in the context of a specific user persona: a small business owner of a construction company who usually works on site.
